LOS ANGELES POLICE DISPATCH AREA

The Burglar Alarm Ordinance Became Effective on November 8, 2004 City of Los Angeles Burglar Alarm Policy and Ordinance quick facts:

  1. Every alarm user within the City of Los Angeles' police dispatch area is required to have a valid police alarm permit, which can be applied for by mail or online, or by calling the Los Angeles Police Commission Alarms Section at (213) 996-1200. The cost of the permit is $34.00 with an annual renewal fee of $30.00. The alarm user is required to provide the alarm company with their permit number and any updated emergency contact information.

  2. The LAPD will respond to two unverified alarm calls per 12-month period. An unverified alarm call is when the alarm user, or a neighbor, or designated person has not independently verified that a security breech has occurred. Upon the third alarm call to LAPD Communications, verification will be required before a patrol unit is dispatched.

  3. If the LAPD responds to an alarm call and it is deemed to be false, and the alarm user has an alarm permit, the fine is $149 for the first offense and an additional $50 will be added for each call thereafter within a 12-month period. For example, the first offense is $149, the second offense is $199, the third offense is $249, the fourth offense is $299, etc.

  4. If the LAPD responds to an alarm call and it is deemed to be false, and the alarm user does not have an alarm permit, the fine is $149 for the first offense along with a $100 penalty. The penalty then grows by $100 for each false alarm call thereafter. For example, the first offense is $249, the second offense is $349, the third offense is $449, the fourth offense is $549, etc.

  5. A false alarm offense, and the fines attached to that offense, may be removed from an alarm user's record each 12 months by attending the Police Commission's "Alarm School."

A seven-day familiarization period is required by the city. This seven-day period begins the day of install. During this period no dispatch requests will be made.
